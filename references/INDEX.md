## Foundations

- [Accessibility](references/foundations/accessibility.md) - Accessible user interfaces empower everyone to have a great experience with your app or game.
- [App icons](references/foundations/app-icons.md) - A unique, memorable icon expresses your app’s or game’s purpose and personality and helps people recognize it at a glance.
- [Branding](references/foundations/branding.md) - Apps and games express their unique brand identity in ways that make them instantly recognizable while feeling at home on the platform and giving people a consistent experience.
- [Color](references/foundations/color.md) - Judicious use of color can enhance communication, evoke your brand, provide visual continuity, communicate status and feedback, and help people understand information.
- [Dark Mode](references/foundations/dark-mode.md) - Dark Mode is a systemwide appearance setting that uses a dark color palette to provide a comfortable viewing experience tailored for low-light environments.
- [Icons](references/foundations/icons.md) - An effective icon is a graphic asset that expresses a single concept in ways people instantly understand.
- [Images](references/foundations/images.md) - To make sure your artwork looks great on all devices you support, learn how the system displays content and how to deliver art at the appropriate scale factors.
- [Immersive experiences](references/foundations/immersive-experiences.md) - In visionOS, you can design apps and games that extend beyond windows and volumes, immersing people in your content.
- [Inclusion](references/foundations/inclusion.md) - Inclusive apps and games put people first by prioritizing respectful communication and presenting content and functionality in ways that everyone can access and understand.
- [Layout](references/foundations/layout.md) - A consistent layout that adapts to various contexts makes your experience more approachable and helps people enjoy their favorite apps and games on all their devices.
- [Materials](references/foundations/materials.md) - A material is a visual effect that creates a sense of depth, layering, and hierarchy between foreground and background elements.
- [Motion](references/foundations/motion.md) - Beautiful, fluid motions bring the interface to life, conveying status, providing feedback and instruction, and enriching the visual experience of your app or game.
- [Privacy](references/foundations/privacy.md) - Privacy is paramount: it’s critical to be transparent about the privacy-related data and resources you require and essential to protect the data people allow you to access.
- [Right to left](references/foundations/right-to-left.md) - Support right-to-left languages like Arabic and Hebrew by reversing your interface as needed to match the reading direction of the related scripts.
- [SF Symbols](references/foundations/sf-symbols.md) - SF Symbols provides thousands of consistent, highly configurable symbols that integrate seamlessly with the San Francisco system font, automatically aligning with text in all weights and sizes.
- [Spatial layout](references/foundations/spatial-layout.md) - Spatial layout techniques help you take advantage of the infinite canvas of Apple Vision Pro and present your content in engaging, comfortable ways.
- [Typography](references/foundations/typography.md) - Your typographic choices can help you display legible text, convey an information hierarchy, communicate important content, and express your brand or style.
- [Writing](references/foundations/writing.md) - The words you choose within your app are an essential part of its user experience.

## Patterns

- [Charting data](references/patterns/charting-data.md) - Presenting data in a chart can help you communicate information with clarity and appeal.
- [Collaboration and sharing](references/patterns/collaboration-and-sharing.md) - Great collaboration and sharing experiences are simple and responsive, letting people engage with the content while communicating effectively with others.
- [Drag and drop](references/patterns/drag-and-drop.md) - Using drag and drop, people can move or duplicate selected photos, text, and other content by dragging the selection from one location to another.
- [Entering data](references/patterns/entering-data.md) - When you need information from people, design ways that make it easy for them to provide it without making mistakes.
- [Feedback](references/patterns/feedback.md) - Feedback helps people know what’s happening, discover what they can do next, understand the results of actions, and avoid mistakes.
- [File management](references/patterns/file-management.md) - Some apps can support documents and files that people expect to manage throughout the system.
- [Going full screen](references/patterns/going-full-screen.md) - iPhone, iPad, and Mac offer full-screen modes that let people expand a window to fill the screen, hiding system controls and providing a distraction-free environment.
- [Launching](references/patterns/launching.md) - A streamlined launch experience helps people start using your app or game immediately.
- [Live-viewing apps](references/patterns/live-viewing-apps.md) - As you design a live-viewing app, prioritize the content and create fun, fluid interactions that encourage immersion in the live-viewing experience.
- [Loading](references/patterns/loading.md) - The best content-loading experience finishes before people become aware of it.
- [Managing accounts](references/patterns/managing-accounts.md) - When it doesn’t create an unnecessary barrier to your experience, an account can be a convenient way for people to access their content and track personal details.
- [Managing notifications](references/patterns/managing-notifications.md) - Notifications can give people timely and important information, whether the device is locked or in use.
- [Modality](references/patterns/modality.md) - Modality is a design technique that presents content in a separate, dedicated mode that prevents interaction with the parent view and requires an explicit action to dismiss.
- [Multitasking](references/patterns/multitasking.md) - Multitasking lets people switch quickly from one app to another, performing tasks in each.
- [Offering help](references/patterns/offering-help.md) - Although the most effective experiences are approachable and intuitive, you can provide contextual help when necessary.
- [Onboarding](references/patterns/onboarding.md) - Onboarding can help people get a quick start using your app or game.
- [Playing audio](references/patterns/playing-audio.md) - People expect rich audio experiences that automatically adjust when the context changes on the device.
- [Playing haptics](references/patterns/playing-haptics.md) - Playing haptics can engage people’s sense of touch and bring their familiarity with the physical world into your app or game.
- [Playing video](references/patterns/playing-video.md) - People expect to enjoy rich video experiences on their devices, regardless of the app or game they’re using.
- [Printing](references/patterns/printing.md) - An iOS, iPadOS, macOS, or visionOS app can integrate system-provided print functionality when it makes sense, presenting custom printer- and document-specific options if necessary.
- [Ratings and reviews](references/patterns/ratings-and-reviews.md) - People often view the ratings and reviews for an app or game before they download it.
- [Searching](references/patterns/searching.md) - People use various search techniques to find content on their device, within an app, and within a document or file.
- [Settings](references/patterns/settings.md) - People expect apps and games to just work, but they also appreciate having ways to customize the experience to fit their needs.
- [Undo and redo](references/patterns/undo-and-redo.md) - Undo and redo gives people easy ways to reverse many types of actions, which can also help people explore and experiment safely as they learn a new interface or task.
- [Workouts](references/patterns/workouts.md) - A great workout or fitness experience encourages people to engage with their current activity and helps them track their progress on their devices.

## Components

- [Action sheets](references/components/action-sheets.md) - An action sheet is a modal view that presents choices related to an action people initiate.
- [Activity rings](references/components/activity-rings.md) - Activity rings show an individual’s daily progress toward Move, Exercise, and Stand goals.
- [Activity views](references/components/activity-views.md) - An activity view - often called a *share sheet* - presents a range of tasks that people can perform in the current context.
- [Alerts](references/components/alerts.md) - An alert gives people critical information they need right away.
- [App Shortcuts](references/components/app-shortcuts.md) - An App Shortcut gives people access to your app’s key functions or content throughout the system.
- [Boxes](references/components/boxes.md) - A box creates a visually distinct group of logically related information and components.
- [Buttons](references/components/buttons.md) - A button initiates an instantaneous action.
- [Charts](references/components/charts.md) - Organize data in a chart to communicate information with clarity and visual appeal.
- [Collections](references/components/collections.md) - A collection manages an ordered set of content and presents it in a customizable and highly visual layout.
- [Color wells](references/components/color-wells.md) - A color well lets people adjust the color of text, shapes, guides, and other onscreen elements.
- [Column views](references/components/column-views.md) - A column view - also called a *browser* - lets people view and navigate a data hierarchy using a series of vertical columns.
- [Combo boxes](references/components/combo-boxes.md) - A combo box combines a text field with a pull-down button in a single control.
- [Complications](references/components/complications.md) - A complication displays timely, relevant information on the watch face, where people can view it each time they raise their wrist.
- [Content](references/components/content.md)
- [Context menus](references/components/context-menus.md) - A context menu provides access to functionality that’s directly related to an item, without cluttering the interface.
- [Controls](references/components/controls.md) - A control provides quick access to a feature of your app from Control Center, the Lock Screen, or the Action button.
- [Digit entry views](references/components/digit-entry-views.md) - A digit entry view fills the entire screen and prompts people to enter a series of digits, like a PIN, using a digit-specific keyboard.
- [Disclosure controls](references/components/disclosure-controls.md) - Disclosure controls reveal and hide information and functionality related to specific controls or views.
- [Dock menus](references/components/dock-menus.md) - On a Mac, people can secondary click an app’s or game’s icon in the Dock to reveal a Dock menu, which presents both system-provided and custom items.
- [Edit menus](references/components/edit-menus.md) - An edit menu lets people make changes to selected content in the current view, in addition to offering related commands like Copy, Select, Translate, and Look Up.
- [Gauges](references/components/gauges.md) - A gauge displays a specific numerical value within a range of values.
- [Home Screen quick actions](references/components/home-screen-quick-actions.md) - Home Screen quick actions give people a way to perform app-specific actions from the Home Screen.
- [Image views](references/components/image-views.md) - An image view displays a single image - or in some cases, an animated sequence of images - on a transparent or opaque background.
- [Image wells](references/components/image-wells.md) - An image well is an editable version of an image view.
- [Labels](references/components/labels.md) - A label is a static piece of text that people can read and often copy, but not edit.
- [Layout and organization](references/components/layout-and-organization.md)
- [Lists and tables](references/components/lists-and-tables.md) - Lists and tables present data in one or more columns of rows.
- [Live Activities](references/components/live-activities.md) - A Live Activity lets people track the progress of an activity, event, or task at a glance.
- [Lockups](references/components/lockups.md) - Lockups combine multiple separate views into a single, interactive unit.
- [Menus](references/components/menus.md) - A menu reveals its options when people interact with it, making it a space-efficient way to present commands in your app or game.
- [Menus and actions](references/components/menus-and-actions.md)
- [Navigation and search](references/components/navigation-and-search.md)
- [Notifications](references/components/notifications.md) - A notification gives people timely, high-value information they can understand at a glance.
- [Ornaments](references/components/ornaments.md) - In visionOS, an ornament presents controls and information related to a window, without crowding or obscuring the window’s contents.
- [Outline views](references/components/outline-views.md) - An outline view presents hierarchical data in a scrolling list of cells that are organized into columns and rows.
- [Page controls](references/components/page-controls.md) - A page control displays a row of indicator images, each of which represents a page in a flat list.
- [Panels](references/components/panels.md) - In a macOS app, a panel typically floats above other open windows providing supplementary controls, options, or information related to the active window or current selection.
- [Path controls](references/components/path-controls.md) - A path control shows the file system path of a selected file or folder.
- [Pickers](references/components/pickers.md) - A picker displays one or more scrollable lists of distinct values that people can choose from.
- [Pop-up buttons](references/components/pop-up-buttons.md) - A pop-up button displays a menu of mutually exclusive options.
- [Popovers](references/components/popovers.md) - A popover is a transient view that appears above other content when people click or tap a control or interactive area.
- [Presentation](references/components/presentation.md)
- [Progress indicators](references/components/progress-indicators.md) - Progress indicators let people know that your app isn’t stalled while it loads content or performs lengthy operations.
- [Pull-down buttons](references/components/pull-down-buttons.md) - A pull-down button displays a menu of items or actions that directly relate to the button’s purpose.
- [Rating indicators](references/components/rating-indicators.md) - A rating indicator uses a series of horizontally arranged graphical symbols - by default, stars - to communicate a ranking level.
- [Scroll views](references/components/scroll-views.md) - A scroll view lets people view content that’s larger than the view’s boundaries by moving the content vertically or horizontally.
- [Search fields](references/components/search-fields.md) - A search field lets people search a collection of content for specific terms they enter.
- [Segmented controls](references/components/segmented-controls.md) - A segmented control is a linear set of two or more segments, each of which functions as a button.
- [Selection and input](references/components/selection-and-input.md)
- [Sheets](references/components/sheets.md) - A sheet helps people perform a scoped task that’s closely related to their current context.
- [Sidebars](references/components/sidebars.md) - A sidebar appears on the leading side of a view and lets people navigate between areas of your app or top-level collections of content, like folders and playlists.
- [Sliders](references/components/sliders.md) - A slider is a horizontal track with a control, called a thumb, that people can adjust between a minimum and maximum value.
- [Snippets](references/components/snippets.md) - When someone performs a task with Siri or an App Shortcut, a snippet shows the result or asks for confirmation.
- [Split views](references/components/split-views.md) - A split view manages the presentation of multiple adjacent panes of content, each of which can contain a variety of components, including tables, collections, images, and custom views.
- [Status](references/components/status.md)
- [Status bars](references/components/status-bars.md) - A status bar appears along the upper edge of the screen and displays information about the device’s current state, like the time, cellular carrier, and battery level.
- [Steppers](references/components/steppers.md) - A stepper is a two-segment control that people use to increase or decrease an incremental value.
- [System experiences](references/components/system-experiences.md)
- [Tab bars](references/components/tab-bars.md) - A tab bar lets people navigate between top-level sections of your app.
- [Tab views](references/components/tab-views.md) - A tab view presents multiple mutually exclusive panes of content in the same area, which people can switch between using a tabbed control.
- [Text fields](references/components/text-fields.md) - A text field is a rectangular area in which people enter or edit small, specific pieces of text.
- [Text views](references/components/text-views.md) - A text view displays multiline, styled text content, which can optionally be editable.
- [The menu bar](references/components/the-menu-bar.md) - On a Mac or an iPad, the menu bar at the top of the screen displays the top-level menus in your app or game.
- [Toggles](references/components/toggles.md) - A toggle lets people choose between a pair of opposing states, like on and off, using a different appearance to indicate each state.
- [Token fields](references/components/token-fields.md) - A token field is a type of text field that can convert text into *tokens* that are easy to select and manipulate.
- [Toolbars](references/components/toolbars.md) - A toolbar provides convenient access to frequently used commands, controls, navigation, and search.
- [Top Shelf](references/components/top-shelf.md) - The Apple TV Home Screen provides an area called Top Shelf, which showcases your content in a rich, engaging way while also giving people access to their favorite apps in the Dock.
- [Virtual keyboards](references/components/virtual-keyboards.md) - On devices without physical keyboards, the system offers various types of virtual keyboards people can use to enter data.
- [Watch faces](references/components/watch-faces.md) - A watch face is a view that people choose as their primary view in watchOS.
- [Web views](references/components/web-views.md) - A web view loads and displays rich web content, such as embedded HTML and websites, directly within your app.
- [Widgets](references/components/widgets.md) - A widget provides quick access to essential information and focused interactions from your app or game in additional contexts.
- [Windows](references/components/windows.md) - A window presents UI views and components in your app or game.
